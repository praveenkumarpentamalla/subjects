**2. Improper Fraction:** Numerator ‚â• Denominator

### **FUNDAMENTAL OPERATIONS**

#### **1. ADDITION OF FRACTIONS**

**A. Like Fractions (Same Denominator)**
```
Add numerators, keep denominator same
a/c + b/c = (a+b)/c
```

**Example:** 2/7 + 3/7 = 5/7

---

**B. Unlike Fractions (Different Denominators)**

**Method 1: LCM Method (Most Common)**

**Steps:**
1. Find LCM of denominators
2. Convert each fraction to equivalent fraction with LCM as denominator
3. Add numerators

**Example:** 1/2 + 1/3
```
LCM(2,3) = 6
1/2 = 3/6
1/3 = 2/6
Sum = 3/6 + 2/6 = 5/6
```

---

**Method 2: Cross-Multiplication Method (Quick for 2 fractions)**

```
a/b + c/d = (ad + bc) / bd
```

**Example:** 1/2 + 1/3
```
= (1√ó3 + 1√ó2) / (2√ó3)
= (3 + 2) / 6
= 5/6
```

**When to use:** Fast for 2 fractions with small numbers

---

#### **2. SUBTRACTION OF FRACTIONS**

**Same rules as addition!**

**Unlike fractions:**
```
a/b - c/d = (ad - bc) / bd
```

**Example:** 3/4 - 1/3
```
= (3√ó3 - 4√ó1) / (4√ó3)
= (9 - 4) / 12

```

#### **3. MULTIPLICATION OF FRACTIONS**

**Simple Rule:** Multiply numerators, multiply denominators

```
a/b √ó c/d = (a√óc) / (b√ód)
```

**Example:** 2/3 √ó 4/5 = 8/15

**SHORTCUT: Cross-Cancellation (Before multiplying)**

**Example:** 2/3 √ó 9/4
```
Cancel 2 and 4: 1/3 √ó 9/2
Cancel 9 and 3: 1/1 √ó 3/2 = 3/2
```

**Saves calculation time!**

#### **4. DIVISION OF FRACTIONS**

**Golden Rule:** Multiply by the reciprocal (flip the second fraction)


**Example:** 2/3 √∑ 4/5

**Memory Hook:** "Division? Flip and multiply!"

### **ADVANCED PROBLEM-SOLVING STRATEGIES**

#### **B. Fraction Word Problems**

**Type 1: Inheritance/Distribution**

**Example:** A man leaves 1/3 of his property to his son, 1/4 to his daughter, and the rest to his wife. If the wife gets ‚Çπ25,000, what was the total property?

**Example:** A can do work in 6 days, B in 8 days. Working together, in how many days?

## 5. COMMON MISTAKES & TRAPS

### **FREQUENT ERRORS STUDENTS MAKE**

#### **Error 1: Adding Fractions Wrong**

‚ùå **Wrong:** 1/2 + 1/3 = 2/5 (adding numerators AND denominators!)

‚úÖ **Correct:** 1/2 + 1/3 = 5/6 (find LCM, convert, then add)

**This is the most common error in exams!**

---

#### **Error 2: Dividing Instead of Multiplying Reciprocal**

‚ùå **Wrong:** (2/3) √∑ (1/4) = 2/12

‚úÖ **Correct:** (2/3) √ó (4/1) = 8/3

---

#### **Error 3: Not Simplifying**

‚ùå **Wrong:** Leaving answer as 12/18

‚úÖ **Correct:** 12/18 = 2/3

**Always simplify to lowest terms!**

---

#### **Error 4: Mixed Number Confusion**

‚ùå **Wrong:** 2¬æ = 2 √ó 3/4 = 6/4

‚úÖ **Correct:** 2¬æ = 2 + 3/4 = 11/4

**Mixed fraction means ADDITION, not multiplication!**

---

#### **Error 5: Order of Operations Error**

‚ùå **Wrong:** 2/3 √ó 1/2 + 1/4 = 2/3 √ó (1/2 + 1/4)

‚úÖ **Correct:** (2/3 √ó 1/2) + 1/4 = 1/3 + 1/4 = 7/12

**Multiplication BEFORE addition!**

---

#### **Error 6: Comparing Fractions Wrong**

‚ùå **Wrong:** 3/5 > 3/7 because 5 > 7

‚úÖ **Correct:** 3/5 > 3/7 because 5 < 7 (bigger denominator = smaller fraction when numerators are same)

---

#### **Error 7: Reciprocal Confusion**

‚ùå **Wrong:** Reciprocal of 3/4 is 3/4

‚úÖ **Correct:** Reciprocal of 3/4 is 4/3

### **TRICK QUESTIONS COMMONLY ASKED**

#### **Trick 1: The "Of" Trap**

**Question:** "What is 2/3 of 3/4 of 120?"

**Trap:** Adding 2/3 and 3/4 first

**Correct:** "Of" means multiply!
```
= 2/3 √ó 3/4 √ó 120 = 60
```

---

#### **Trick 2: The Complement Trap**

**Question:** "A spends 3/7 of his money. What fraction is LEFT?"

**Trap:** Forgetting to subtract from 1

**Correct:** Left = 1 - 3/7 = 4/7

---

#### **Trick 3: The Fraction Exceeds Trap**

**Question:** "2/5 of a number exceeds 1/3 by 11. Find the number."

**Trap:** Setting up equation wrong

**Correct:**
```
2/5 √ó N = 1/3 √ó N + 11
(2/5 - 1/3)N = 11
(6-5)/15 √ó N = 11
N/15 = 11
N = 165
```

---

#### **Trick 4: The Unit Fraction Sum**

**Question:** "1/2 + 1/3 + 1/6 = ?"

**Trap:** Adding numerators (1+1+1=3) and denominators (2+3+6=11) ‚Üí 3/11

**Correct:** LCM(2,3,6) = 6
```
3/6 + 2/6 + 1/6 = 6/6 = 1
```

---

#### **Trick 5: The Order Reversal**

**Question:** "If x = 3/4, what is 1/x?"

**Trap:** Calculating 1-x = 1/4

**Correct:** 1/x = 4/3 (reciprocal, not complement!)

### **HOW TO AVOID CALCULATION MISTAKES**

#### **Strategy 1: Cross-Cancel Before Multiplying**

Always look for common factors before multiplying:
```

```

#### **Strategy 2: Check by Estimation**

Before exact calculation, estimate:
- 2/3 ‚âà 0.67
- 3/4 = 0.75
- Sum should be ‚âà 1.42

If your answer is 2.5, something's wrong!

---

#### **Strategy 3: Verify with Cross-Multiplication**

After comparing fractions, verify:
```

21>20, so 3/5 > 4/7 ‚úì
```

---

#### **Strategy 4: Always Convert Mixed to Improper First**

Before any operation with mixed fractions:
```
2¬Ω √ó 1‚Öì ‚Üí 5/2 √ó 4/3 ‚Üí then calculate
```

#### **Strategy 5: Check Final Simplification**

Check if answer can be simplified:

- Both divisible by 3? Divide by 3
- Both end in 0 or 5? Divide by 5
