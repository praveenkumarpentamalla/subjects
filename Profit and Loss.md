# MASTERING PROFIT AND LOSS FOR COMPETITIVE GOVERNMENT EXAMS

## 1. FOUNDATION TO ADVANCED COVERAGE

### **BASIC CONCEPTS & DEFINITIONS**

**Cost Price (CP):** The price at which an article is purchased.

**Selling Price (SP):** The price at which an article is sold.

**Profit:** When SP > CP
- Profit = SP - CP

**Loss:** When CP > SP
- Loss = CP - SP

**Marked Price (MP):** The price printed on the article (list price).

**Discount:** Reduction given on the marked price.
- Discount = MP - SP

**Real-world example:** You buy a phone for ‚Çπ10,000 (CP), the shopkeeper has marked it at ‚Çπ15,000 (MP), gives you ‚Çπ2,000 discount, so you pay ‚Çπ13,000 (SP). His profit = ‚Çπ3,000.

### **INTERMEDIATE LEVEL TECHNIQUES**

**Successive Discounts:** When multiple discounts are applied one after another.

**False Weights:** Shopkeeper uses faulty weights to cheat.

**Dishonest Dealings:** Mixing cheaper and costlier items, or buying at one rate and selling at another while claiming different weights.

**Partnership in Business:** Multiple people invest in a business and share profit/loss.

### **ADVANCED PROBLEM-SOLVING STRATEGIES**

**Transaction-based Problems:** Multiple buying and selling operations.

**Replacement of Items:** Shopkeeper replaces good quality with bad quality.

**Percentage Change Problems:** Finding overall effect when prices fluctuate.

## 2. ALL IMPORTANT FORMULAS & SHORTCUTS

### **BASIC FORMULAS**

**1. Profit Percentage:**
```
Profit% = (Profit/CP) √ó 100
Profit% = [(SP - CP)/CP] √ó 100
```

**2. Loss Percentage:**
```
Loss% = (Loss/CP) √ó 100
Loss% = [(CP - SP)/CP] √ó 100
```

**3. Finding SP when CP and Profit% are given:**
```
SP = CP √ó (100 + Profit%)/100
```

**4. Finding SP when CP and Loss% are given:**
```
SP = CP √ó (100 - Loss%)/100
```

**5. Finding CP when SP and Profit% are given:**
```
CP = SP √ó 100/(100 + Profit%)
```

**6. Finding CP when SP and Loss% are given:**
```
CP = SP √ó 100/(100 - Loss%)
```

### **DISCOUNT FORMULAS**

**7. Discount Percentage:**
```
Discount% = (Discount/MP) √ó 100
```

**8. SP after Discount:**
```
SP = MP √ó (100 - Discount%)/100
```

**9. Successive Discounts (D‚ÇÅ% followed by D‚ÇÇ%):**
```
Net Discount% = D‚ÇÅ + D‚ÇÇ - (D‚ÇÅ √ó D‚ÇÇ)/100
```

**For three discounts:**
```
Net% = D‚ÇÅ + D‚ÇÇ + D‚ÇÉ - (D‚ÇÅD‚ÇÇ + D‚ÇÇD‚ÇÉ + D‚ÇÉD‚ÇÅ)/100 + (D‚ÇÅD‚ÇÇD‚ÇÉ)/10000
```

### **GOLDEN SHORTCUTS**

**SHORTCUT 1: Percentage to Fraction Conversion**
- 10% = 1/10
- 20% = 1/5
- 25% = 1/4
- 33‚Öì% = 1/3
- 50% = 1/2
- 66‚Öî% = 2/3

**SHORTCUT 2: Quick SP Calculation**
If Profit% = 25%, then SP = CP √ó 5/4 (faster than calculating 125/100)

**SHORTCUT 3: Ratio Method**
If an article is sold at a profit of 20%:
- CP : SP = 100 : 120 = 5 : 6

If sold at a loss of 20%:
- CP : SP = 100 : 80 = 5 : 4

**SHORTCUT 4: When Two Items are Sold at Same SP**

One at x% profit and another at x% loss:
```
Overall Loss% = (x/10)¬≤
```
(Always a loss, remember this!)

**Example:** One item at 10% profit, another at 10% loss
- Overall loss = (10/10)¬≤ = 1%

**SHORTCUT 5: False Weight Formula**

If a shopkeeper uses weight 'x' gm instead of 1000 gm:
```
Profit% = [(1000 - x)/x] √ó 100
```

**SHORTCUT 6: Dishonest Dealer**

Claims to sell at CP but uses false weight:
```
Profit% = [(True Weight - False Weight)/False Weight] √ó 100
```

**SHORTCUT 7: Successive Discounts Quick Method**

For 20% and 10% discounts:
- Think: 80% of 80% = 0.8 √ó 0.9 = 0.72 = 72%
- So net price = 72% of MP
- Discount = 28%

**SHORTCUT 8: MP to CP Relationship**

If MP is x% above CP and discount is y%:
```
Profit% = [(100 + x)(100 - y)/100] - 100
```

**SHORTCUT 9: Percentage Change**

If price increases by x% then decreases by x%:
```
Net change = -x¬≤/100 (always a decrease)
```

**SHORTCUT 10: Break-Even Analysis**

To have no profit no loss after giving discount on marked price:
```
If MP is x% above CP, maximum discount that can be given = x/(100 + x) √ó 100
```

**The "False Weight" Pattern:**

### **MEMORY HOOKS THAT LAST LIFELONG**

**Hook 1: "The SP-CP Relationship"**
Think of a see-saw:
- SP up, CP down = Profit
- CP up, SP down = Loss

**Hook 2: "The 100 Rule"**
Every percentage formula has 100 in it. CP is your base (100), everything is calculated from there.

**Hook 3: "Discount is a Gift"**
Discount always reduces from Marked Price to reach Selling Price. It's a gift to customer from seller.

**Hook 4: "Successive = Multiplication"**
Successive discounts multiply, they don't add.
- 20% off then 10% off = 0.8 √ó 0.9 = 0.72 (28% total discount)

**Hook 5: "Same Selling = Loss Ball"**
When two items sold at same SP with equal profit% and loss%, you're always in the loss (like dropping a ball - it falls!)

## 5. COMMON MISTAKES & TRAPS

### **FREQUENT ERRORS STUDENTS MAKE**

**Mistake 1: Confusing MP with CP**
- MP is the labeled price (not what seller paid)
- Discount is always on MP, not CP
- Profit/Loss is always calculated on CP

**Mistake 2: Adding Successive Discounts Directly**
- 20% + 10% ‚â† 30%
- Must use formula or multiplication method

**Mistake 3: Wrong Denominator in Percentage**
- Profit% = Profit/CP √ó 100 (NOT on SP)
- Discount% = Discount/MP √ó 100 (NOT on SP)
- Always remember the base!

**Mistake 4: Same SP Trap**
- When two items sold at same SP with x% profit and x% loss
- Students think it's break-even
- Reality: Always a loss = (x/10)¬≤%

**Mistake 5: False Weight Confusion**
- Formula is (1000 - false weight)/false weight
- NOT (1000 - false weight)/1000

**Mistake 6: Percentage Increase-Decrease**
- If price increases 20% then decreases 20%
- Final price is NOT same as original
- Always results in net decrease = x¬≤/100

### **TRICK QUESTIONS COMMONLY ASKED**

**Trap 1:** "A man claims to sell at cost price but uses 800g weight instead of 1kg. Find profit%"
- Students forget: Selling at CP doesn't mean no profit when using false weight!
- Answer: (200/800) √ó 100 = 25% profit

**Trap 2:** "MP is 25% above CP. To have no profit no loss, what discount to give?"
- Students give 25% (wrong!)
- Correct: 25/(100+25) √ó 100 = 20%

**Trap 3:** "Two articles sold at ‚Çπ1000 each, one at 10% profit, other at 10% loss"
- Students think break-even (wrong!)
- Correct: 1% loss always

**Trap 4:** "If CP of 15 items = SP of 12 items"
- Students calculate (15-12)/15 = 20% (wrong denominator!)
- Correct: Profit% = (3/12) √ó 100 = 25%

**Trap 5:** "Successive discounts of 40%, 20%, 10%"
- Students add: 70% (wrong!)
- Correct: 0.6 √ó 0.8 √ó 0.9 = 0.432 ‚Üí 56.8% discount

### **HOW TO AVOID CALCULATION MISTAKES**

**1. Write CP, SP, MP clearly**
- Label what you're finding
- Don't mix up values

**2. Use assumed values (100 or 1000)**
- Makes calculation easier
- Reduces errors

**3. Check if answer makes sense**
- 200% profit on groceries? Unlikely!
- Helps catch errors

**4. Use approximations in MCQs**
- Don't waste time on exact decimals if options are far apart

**5. Practice mental math for common fractions**
- 1/4 = 25%, 1/3 = 33.33%, 2/3 = 66.67%
- Memorize these
