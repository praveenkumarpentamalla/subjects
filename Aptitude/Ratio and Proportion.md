## PART 1: FOUNDATION TO ADVANCED COVERAGE

### **LEVEL 1: BASIC FOUNDATIONS**

#### **What is Ratio?**

A **ratio** is a comparison between two quantities of the same kind.

**Real-world example:** If you have 2 apples and 3 oranges, the ratio of apples to oranges is 2:3

**Key notation:** a:b (read as "a is to b")

#### **What is Proportion?**

A **proportion** states that two ratios are equal.

**Example:** 2:3 = 4:6 (this is a proportion)

**Notation:** a:b :: c:d (read as "a is to b as c is to d")
**OR** a/b = c/d

---

#### **FUNDAMENTAL CONCEPTS**

**1. Components of a Ratio**

For ratio a:b
- **a** = First term (antecedent)
- **b** = Second term (consequent)

**Example:** In 3:5
- 3 is the antecedent
- 5 is the consequent

**2. Types of Ratios**

**Duplicate Ratio:** a¬≤:b¬≤ is the duplicate ratio of a:b
- Example: Duplicate of 2:3 is 4:9

**Triplicate Ratio:** a¬≥:b¬≥ is the triplicate ratio of a:b
- Example: Triplicate of 2:3 is 8:27

**Sub-duplicate Ratio:** ‚àöa:‚àöb is the sub-duplicate ratio of a:b
- Example: Sub-duplicate of 4:9 is 2:3

**Sub-triplicate Ratio:** ‚àõa:‚àõb is the sub-triplicate ratio of a:b
- Example: Sub-triplicate of 8:27 is 2:3

**Compound Ratio:** (ac):(bd) is compound of a:b and c:d
- Example: Compound of 2:3 and 4:5 is (2√ó4):(3√ó5) = 8:15

**Inverse/Reciprocal Ratio:** b:a is inverse of a:b
- Example: Inverse of 3:4 is 4:3

**3. Properties of Ratios**

‚úì **A ratio has no units** (it's just a comparison)
‚úì **Ratio remains same if both terms multiplied/divided by same number**
  - 2:3 = 4:6 = 6:9 = 10:15
‚úì **Order matters:** 2:3 ‚â† 3:2
‚úì **Both terms must be in same units before comparing**

**4. Components of a Proportion**

For a:b :: c:d (or a/b = c/d)

- **a and d** = Extremes (outer terms)
- **b and c** = Means (middle terms)

**GOLDEN RULE:** **Product of extremes = Product of means**
- **a √ó d = b √ó c** ‚Üê This is THE most important property!

**Example:** In 2:3 :: 4:6
- Extremes: 2 and 6 ‚Üí Product = 12
- Means: 3 and 4 ‚Üí Product = 12 ‚úì

---

#### **5. Types of Proportions**

**a) Direct Proportion (Varies Directly)**

When one quantity increases, the other also increases in the same ratio.

**Symbol:** ‚àù (varies as)

**Example:** 
- More workers ‚Üí More work done
- More speed ‚Üí More distance covered
- More items ‚Üí More cost

**Formula:** If x ‚àù y, then x/y = k (constant)
Or: x‚ÇÅ/y‚ÇÅ = x‚ÇÇ/y‚ÇÇ

**b) Inverse Proportion (Varies Inversely)**

When one quantity increases, the other decreases in the same ratio.

**Example:**
- More workers ‚Üí Less time needed
- More speed ‚Üí Less time taken
- More pipes ‚Üí Less time to fill

**Formula:** If x ‚àù 1/y, then xy = k (constant)
Or: x‚ÇÅy‚ÇÅ = x‚ÇÇy‚ÇÇ

**Memory Hook:** "Direct = Divide, Inverse = Multiply"

---

#### **6. Continued Proportion**

Three quantities a, b, c are in continued proportion if:
**a:b :: b:c**

This means: **b¬≤ = ac**

**Example:** 2, 4, 8 are in continued proportion
- Check: 4¬≤ = 16 = 2 √ó 8 ‚úì

**Mean Proportional:** If a:b :: b:c, then **b = ‚àö(ac)**
- b is called the mean proportional between a and c

**Third Proportional:** If a:b :: b:c, then **c = b¬≤/a**
- c is called the third proportional to a and b

**Fourth Proportional:** If a:b :: c:x, then **x = bc/a**
- x is called the fourth proportional to a, b, and c

### **LEVEL 2: INTERMEDIATE TECHNIQUES**

#### **7. Componendo and Dividendo Rules**

These are powerful shortcuts for competitive exams!

**Given:** a/b = c/d

**Componendo:** (a+b)/b = (c+d)/d

**Dividendo:** (a-b)/b = (c-d)/d

**Componendo-Dividendo (Combined):** 
**(a+b)/(a-b) = (c+d)/(c-d)**

**Example:** If (x+y)/(x-y) = 5/3
Using componendo-dividendo:
- Let a = x+y, b = x-y
- Then 2x/2y = 8/2
- x/y = 4/1
- **x:y = 4:1**

**Memory:** "Add on top, subtract on bottom"

---

#### **8. Ratio Distribution Problems**

**Type 1: Two-part division**

If a sum S is divided in ratio a:b, then:
- **First part = S √ó a/(a+b)**
- **Second part = S √ó b/(a+b)**

**Example:** Divide ‚Çπ500 in ratio 2:3
- First part = 500 √ó 2/(2+3) = 500 √ó 2/5 = ‚Çπ200
- Second part = 500 √ó 3/5 = ‚Çπ300

**Type 2: Three-part division**

If sum S is divided in ratio a:b:c, then:
- **First part = S √ó a/(a+b+c)**
- **Second part = S √ó b/(a+b+c)**
- **Third part = S √ó c/(a+b+c)**

**Example:** Divide ‚Çπ600 in ratio 1:2:3
- Parts: 600√ó1/6, 600√ó2/6, 600√ó3/6 = ‚Çπ100, ‚Çπ200, ‚Çπ300

**Shortcut Formula:** 
Part = (Total √ó That ratio term)/(Sum of all ratio terms)

#### **9. Partnership Problems (Special Ratio Application)**

**Simple Partnership (Time same, Investment different)**

Profit ratio = Investment ratio

**Example:** A invests ‚Çπ3000, B invests ‚Çπ5000
Profit ratio = 3000:5000 = **3:5**

**Compound Partnership (Time different, Investment different)**

Profit ratio = (Investment‚ÇÅ √ó Time‚ÇÅ):(Investment‚ÇÇ √ó Time‚ÇÇ)

**Example:** 
- A invests ‚Çπ3000 for 4 months
- B invests ‚Çπ4000 for 3 months
Profit ratio = (3000√ó4):(4000√ó3) = 12000:12000 = **1:1**

**Memory:** "Money √ó Months = Merit"

---

#### **10. Age Ratio Problems**

**Key Pattern:**

If present ages are in ratio a:b, and after n years in ratio c:d, then:

**Formula:** (Age‚ÇÅ + n)/(Age‚ÇÇ + n) = c/d

**Approach:**
1. Let present ages = ax and bx
2. After n years: (ax+n)/(bx+n) = c/d
3. Cross multiply and solve for x

**Example:** Present ages in ratio 3:5. After 10 years, ratio becomes 2:3.

Let ages = 3x and 5x
After 10 years: (3x+10)/(5x+10) = 2/3
Cross multiply: 3(3x+10) = 2(5x+10)
9x + 30 = 10x + 20
x = 10

Present ages: 30 and 50 years

### **LEVEL 3: ADVANCED PROBLEM-SOLVING**

#### **11. Mixture and Alligation (Advanced Ratio)**

**Alligation Rule:** 

When two ingredients of prices P‚ÇÅ and P‚ÇÇ are mixed to get mean price M:

**Ratio = (M - P‚ÇÇ):(P‚ÇÅ - M)**

**Visual Method (Alligation Cross):**

```
        P‚ÇÅ (Cheaper)
              \
               M (Mean)
              /
        P‚ÇÇ (Dearer)

Ratio = (M-P‚ÇÇ):(P‚ÇÅ-M)
      = (Dearer - Mean):(Mean - Cheaper)
```

**Example:** Mix rice at ‚Çπ30/kg with rice at ‚Çπ50/kg to get mixture at ‚Çπ40/kg

```
        30
          \
           40
          /
        50

Ratio = (50-40):(40-30) = 10:10 = 1:1
```

Mix in equal quantities!

**Memory:** "Dearer minus Mean, Mean minus Cheaper"

#### **12. Replacement Problems (Advanced)**

**Formula:** After n operations of replacement:

**Final quantity = Initial √ó (1 - R/T)‚Åø**

Where:
- R = Quantity replaced each time
- T = Total quantity
- n = Number of operations

**Example:** 100L mixture, 10L removed and replaced with water each time. After 3 operations, original liquid remaining?

= 100 √ó (1 - 10/100)¬≥
= 100 √ó (0.9)¬≥
= 100 √ó 0.729
= **72.9 liters**

#### **13. Income and Expenditure Ratios**

**Standard Problems:**

If incomes in ratio a:b and expenses in ratio c:d, then:

**Savings ratio** can be found using:
Savings‚ÇÅ:Savings‚ÇÇ = (Income‚ÇÅ - Expense‚ÇÅ):(Income‚ÇÇ - Expense‚ÇÇ)

**Common Question Type:**

"Incomes in ratio 4:5, expenses in ratio 2:3. If A saves ‚Çπ1000, find B's savings"

**Method:**
Let incomes = 4x and 5x
Let expenses = 2y and 3y
A's savings: 4x - 2y = 1000
For B: 5x - 3y = ?

Need additional constraint to solve!

#### **14. Compound Ratios with Multiple Variables**

**Type:** A:B = 2:3, B:C = 4:5. Find A:B:C

**Method:** Make B equal in both ratios

A:B = 2:3 = 8:12 (multiply by 4)
B:C = 4:5 = 12:15 (multiply by 3)

Combined: **A:B:C = 8:12:15**

**Shortcut:** 
- LCM of B's values = LCM(3,4) = 12
- Adjust both ratios to make B = 12

#### **15. Variation Problems**

**Direct Variation:** x ‚àù y means x = ky

**Inverse Variation:** x ‚àù 1/y means x = k/y

**Joint Variation:** x ‚àù yz means x = kyz

**Combined Variation:** x ‚àù y/z means x = ky/z

**Example:** Work ‚àù (Workers √ó Time)
If work is constant: Workers ‚àù 1/Time (inverse)

### **A. FUNDAMENTAL FORMULAS**

**1. Basic Ratio**
- Ratio a:b = a/b

**2. Proportion Properties**
- a:b :: c:d ‚üπ a/b = c/d
- Product of extremes = Product of means
- a/b = c/d ‚üπ a/c = b/d (Alternendo)
- a/b = c/d ‚üπ b/a = d/c (Invertendo)

**3. Componendo-Dividendo**
- If a/b = c/d, then (a+b)/(a-b) = (c+d)/(c-d)

- If a:b :: b:c, then b = ‚àö(ac)

### **MNEMONIC 1: Proportion Property**

**"EXTREME MEANS EQUAL"**

For a:b :: c:d

### **MNEMONIC 2: Componendo-Dividendo**

**"ADD on TOP, SUBTRACT on BOTTOM"**

If a/b = c/d, then:
- **ADD:** (a+b)/(c+d) [Wrong!]
- Correct: (a+b)/b = (c+d)/d

Better mnemonic: **"TOGETHER over ORIGINAL, APART over ORIGINAL"**

Componendo: (a+b)/b [adding a to numerator]
Dividendo: (a-b)/b [subtracting b from numerator]

### **MNEMONIC 3: Direct vs Inverse Proportion**

**"DIRECT = DIVIDE, INVERSE = MULTIPLY"**

**Direct:** x‚ÇÅ/x‚ÇÇ = y‚ÇÅ/y‚ÇÇ (cross-divide)

**Inverse:** x‚ÇÅ √ó y‚ÇÅ = x‚ÇÇ √ó y‚ÇÇ (cross-multiply)

**Example memory:**
- More workers ‚Üí More work = **DIRECT** = **DIVIDE**
- More workers ‚Üí Less time = **INVERSE** = **MULTIPLY**

### **MNEMONIC 4: Types of Proportionals**

**Memory hook:**
- **Mean** = between two (geometric mean)

## PART 5: COMMON MISTAKES & TRAPS

### **MISTAKE 1: Confusing Ratio with Fraction**

**Wrong thinking:** Ratio 3:5 means 3/8 and 5/8 ‚úó

**Right:** Ratio 3:5 means:
- First part = 3/(3+5) = 3/8 of total ‚úì
- Second part = 5/8 of total ‚úì

**But ratio itself is 3:5, not 3/8:5/8**

---

### **MISTAKE 2: Forgetting Common Denominator in Ratio Comparisons**

**Problem:** Which is greater: 2:3 or 3:4?

**Wrong:** 2 < 3, so 2:3 is smaller ‚úó

**Right:** Convert to fractions:
2:3 = 2/3 ‚âà 0.667
3:4 = 3/4 = 0.75

**3:4 is greater** ‚úì

**Or:** Cross multiply: 2√ó4 = 8, 3√ó3 = 9
Since 9 > 8, therefore 3:4 > 2:3

---

### **MISTAKE 3: Wrong Order in Proportion**

**Problem:** If 3:4 :: 6:x, find x

**Wrong:** x = 3√ó4/6 = 2 ‚úó

**Right:** a:b :: c:d means a/b = c/d
So: 3/4 = 6/x
x = 6√ó4/3 = **8** ‚úì

**Remember:** Cross multiply correctly: **ad = bc**

---

### **MISTAKE 4: Inverse Proportion Confusion**

**Problem:** 6 workers finish in 10 days. How many days for 15 workers?

**Wrong:** Days = 10√ó15/6 = 25 days ‚úó (used direct proportion!)

**Right:** More workers = Less time (inverse!)
Days = 6√ó10/15 = **4 days** ‚úì

**Formula:** W‚ÇÅD‚ÇÅ = W‚ÇÇD‚ÇÇ

---

### **MISTAKE 5: Componendo-Dividendo Misapplication**

**Problem:** If (x+y)/(x-y) = 5/3, find x/y

**Wrong:** x/y = 5/3 ‚úó

**Right:** Use reverse C-D formula:
x/y = (5+3)/(5-3) = 8/2 = **4/1** ‚úì

**Don't skip the formula!**

---

### **MISTAKE 6: Partnership Time Calculation Error**

**Problem:** A invests for 4 months, B for 6 months. Equal investment. Profit ratio?

**Wrong:** 4:6 = 2:3 ‚úó (forgot this is correct!)

**Actually this is CORRECT:** 2:3 ‚úì

**Common mistake:** Thinking equal investment means equal profit (forgetting time factor!)

---

### **MISTAKE 7: Alligation Direction Confusion**

**Problem:** Mix ‚Çπ20 and ‚Çπ30 items to get ‚Çπ26

**Wrong:** Ratio = (26-20):(30-26) = 6:4 ‚úó

**Right:** Ratio = (30-26):(26-20) = 4:6 = **2:3** ‚úì

**Problem:** 100L, remove 10L three times

### **HOW TO AVOID THESE MISTAKES**

1. **Read question twice** - especially check what's being asked (A:B or B:A?)

2. **Identify proportion type** - Direct or Inverse? (huge difference!)

3. **Write formula before solving** - prevents confusion

4. **Check units** - all quantities in same units before ratio

5. **Verify with sum** - if dividing, parts should add to total

6. **Cross-multiply to check** - quick verification of proportions

7. **Don't rush componendo-dividendo** - write formula explicitly

8. **Draw alligation cross** - visual prevents inversion errors

9. **For ages: mark timeline** - "present", "3 years ago", "5 years hence"

10. **Sanity check** - does answer make logical sense?
